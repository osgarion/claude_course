About this model — what it is, where it's from, how good it is

What is D2T RA? "Difficult-to-treat" rheumatoid arthritis (EULAR definition) — RA that keeps failing multiple biologic / targeted-synthetic DMARD therapies.

What this tool does. Estimates the probability that a patient belongs to the D2T class rather than sustained remission, from three clinical measures (DAS28-ESR, CRP, treatment duration) plus two plasma proteins (ORM1, FSTL1), using a Firth penalised logistic regression.

Where it comes from. A teaching reimplementation of the model in Baloun et al., "Towards prospective identification of difficult-to-treat rheumatoid arthritis: large-scale plasma proteomics reveals ORM1 as a circulating predictor" (Institute of Rheumatology, Prague). Discovery cohort 198 patients (108 remission / 90 D2T); ELISA validation cohort n = 96 (38 D2T / 58 remission).

How good is it? In the paper, ORM1 alone reached AUC ≈ 0.86; the augmented model (clinical baseline + ORM1) reached AUC ≈ 0.93. ORM1 is an acute-phase protein measurable by standard ELISA — the study's key validated finding.

Important limits. Teaching only, not for clinical decisions. This app does cross-sectional classification (D2T vs remission); the paper's real contribution is prospective prediction from samples taken a median of 2.4–5.8 years before diagnosis. Small, single-centre cohorts — treat every number as illustrative.
